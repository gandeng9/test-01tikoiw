How To Schedule Jobs In Tableau Server

Introduction

Tableau Server is a powerful tool that allows you to share and collaborate on data visualizations with your team. One of the most important features of Tableau Server is the ability to schedule jobs. Scheduling jobs can save you time and ensure that your data is always up-to-date. In this article, we will explore how to schedule jobs in Tableau Server.

Step 1: Create a Data Source

To schedule a job in Tableau Server, you first need to create a data source. A data source is a collection of data that you can use to create visualizations. To create a data source, go to the Data menu and select “New Data Source”. Follow the prompts to connect to your data and create your data source.

Step 2: Create a Workbook

Once you have created your data source, you can create a workbook. A workbook is a collection of visualizations that you can share with others. To create a workbook, go to the “Workbook” menu and select “New Workbook”. Follow the prompts to create your workbook.

Step 3: Create a View

Once you have created your workbook, you can create a view. A view is a specific visualization that you want to share with others. To create a view, drag and drop your data onto the worksheet and create your visualization.

Step 4: Schedule a Job

Once you have created your view, you can schedule a job to update your data on a regular basis. To schedule a job, go to the “Server” menu and select “Schedule Refresh”. Follow the prompts to schedule your job. You can choose to refresh your data on a specific schedule, such as daily, weekly, or monthly.

Step 5: Monitor Your Job

Once you have scheduled your job, you can monitor its progress. To monitor your job, go to the “Server” menu and select “Status”. You can view the status of your job and make any necessary changes.

Tips for Scheduling Jobs in Tableau Server

- Make sure that your data source is up-to-date before scheduling a job. - Schedule your job during off-peak hours to avoid slowing down your server. - Test your job before scheduling it to ensure that it is working correctly. - Monitor your job regularly to ensure that it is running smoothly.

Conclusion

Scheduling jobs in Tableau Server is a powerful feature that can save you time and ensure that your data is always up-to-date. By following these simple steps, you can easily schedule jobs in Tableau Server and share your visualizations with your team. Remember to monitor your jobs regularly to ensure that they are running smoothly.
