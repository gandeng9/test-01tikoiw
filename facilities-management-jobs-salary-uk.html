Facilities Management Jobs Salary In Uk: A Comprehensive Guide In 2023

Introduction

If you're interested in pursuing a career in facilities management in the UK, you're probably wondering what kind of salary you can expect. Facilities management encompasses a wide range of roles, from overseeing the maintenance of buildings and equipment to managing the budgets for facilities projects. In this article, we'll take a look at the average salaries for facilities management jobs in the UK, as well as some factors that can impact your earning potential.

The Average Salary for Facilities Management Jobs in the UK

According to data from the Office for National Statistics, the average salary for facilities management jobs in the UK in 2023 is £36,000 per year. This figure can vary depending on the specific role you're in, as well as factors such as your level of experience and the location of your job.

Factors That Can Impact Your Earning Potential

One of the biggest factors that can impact your earning potential in facilities management is your level of experience. As you gain more experience in the field, you may be able to take on more senior roles that come with higher salaries. Another factor that can impact your salary is the location of your job. Facilities management jobs in London, for example, tend to pay higher salaries than those in other parts of the UK. This is due to the higher cost of living in the capital.

Types of Facilities Management Roles and Salaries

Facilities management encompasses a wide range of roles, and the salaries for these roles can vary significantly. Here are some examples of different facilities management roles and their average salaries in the UK: - Facilities Manager: £40,000 - £60,000 per year - Facilities Coordinator: £25,000 - £35,000 per year - Facilities Assistant: £20,000 - £25,000 per year - Facilities Engineer: £35,000 - £50,000 per year - Facilities Director: £70,000 - £100,000+ per year

How to Increase Your Earning Potential in Facilities Management

If you're looking to increase your earning potential in facilities management, there are a few things you can do. One of the most important is to gain relevant qualifications and certifications. This can demonstrate to employers that you have the skills and knowledge needed to excel in your role. Another way to increase your earning potential is to gain experience in a specific area of facilities management. For example, if you have experience managing budgets for facilities projects, you may be able to take on more senior roles that come with higher salaries.

Conclusion

If you're considering a career in facilities management in the UK, it's important to have a good understanding of the average salaries for different roles. While there is some variation in salaries depending on factors such as location and experience, the average salary for facilities management jobs in the UK in 2023 is £36,000 per year. By gaining relevant qualifications and experience, you can increase your earning potential and take on more senior roles in the field.
