What Are Whole Foods?

Whole foods refer to foods that are minimally processed or unprocessed, and are free from additives, preservatives, and artificial ingredients. They are typically natural, organic, and sustainably sourced. Some examples of whole foods include fresh fruits and vegetables, whole grains, nuts, seeds, and legumes.

The Benefits of Whole Foods

Whole foods are known to have numerous health benefits. They are rich in nutrients, vitamins, and minerals, and are low in calories, saturated fats, and sugars. Eating a diet rich in whole foods can help reduce the risk of chronic diseases such as diabetes, heart disease, and cancer.
