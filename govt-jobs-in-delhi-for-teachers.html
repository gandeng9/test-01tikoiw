Government Teaching Jobs In Delhi: A Comprehensive Guide

Best jobs Tips and References website . Search anything about jobs Ideas in this website.

Introduction

Teaching has always been a noble profession, and the demand for teachers in India is always high. If you are looking for a government teaching job in Delhi, then you are in the right place. In this article, we will discuss everything you need to know about government teaching jobs in Delhi.

Eligibility Criteria

Before applying for a government teaching job in Delhi, you must fulfill the eligibility criteria. The candidate must have a Bachelor's degree in Education (B.Ed) or a Master's degree in the relevant subject. The age limit for most of the teaching posts is between 21 to 40 years. The candidate must also clear the Central Teacher Eligibility Test (CTET) or State Teacher Eligibility Test (STET) to be eligible for the post.

Types of Teaching Jobs

Primary Teacher (PRT)

A primary teacher is responsible for teaching students from class 1 to class 5. The eligibility for this post is a Bachelor's degree in Education (B.Ed) and clearing the CTET exam.

Trained Graduate Teacher (TGT)

A trained graduate teacher is responsible for teaching students from class 6 to class 10. The eligibility for this post is a Bachelor's degree in Education (B.Ed) and clearing the CTET exam.

Post Graduate Teacher (PGT)

A post-graduate teacher is responsible for teaching students from class 11 to class 12. The eligibility for this post is a Master's degree in the relevant subject, a Bachelor's degree in Education (B.Ed), and clearing the CTET exam.

Selection Process

The selection process for government teaching jobs in Delhi usually consists of a written examination followed by an interview. The written exam consists of questions on general knowledge, subject-specific knowledge, and teaching aptitude. The final selection is based on the performance in the written exam and the interview.

Salary and Benefits

The salary for government teaching jobs in Delhi varies depending on the post and the experience of the candidate. The starting salary for a primary teacher (PRT) is around Rs. 35,000 per month, while a post-graduate teacher (PGT) can earn up to Rs. 70,000 per month. In addition to a good salary, government teachers in Delhi also get benefits such as health insurance, pension, and paid leaves.

How to Apply

To apply for a government teaching job in Delhi, you need to keep an eye on the official websites of the Delhi Subordinate Services Selection Board (DSSSB) and the Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan (KVS). The application process usually starts with filling an online application form, followed by the submission of documents and fees. Make sure to read the instructions carefully before applying.

Conclusion

Government teaching jobs in Delhi are highly competitive, but they are also rewarding. If you fulfill the eligibility criteria and have a passion for teaching, then you should definitely apply for these posts. We hope this article provided you with all the necessary information about government teaching jobs in Delhi.
