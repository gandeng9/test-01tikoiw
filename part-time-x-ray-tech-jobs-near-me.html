What is an X-ray Tech?

An X-ray tech, also known as a radiologic technologist or radiographer, is a healthcare professional who uses X-ray equipment to produce images of a patient's internal organs and bones. They work under the supervision of a physician and are responsible for positioning patients, adjusting the equipment, and ensuring that the images produced are of high quality.

Why Choose a Part-Time X-Ray Tech Job?

There are many reasons why you may want to choose a part-time X-ray tech job. Perhaps you are a student looking for a job that will allow you to continue your studies while gaining valuable experience in the healthcare field. Or maybe you are a stay-at-home parent who needs a job that offers flexibility. Whatever your situation may be, a part-time X-ray tech job can be a great option.

There are many places where you can look for part-time X-ray tech jobs. Here are some of the most popular options:

  • Online job boards such as Indeed, Glassdoor, and Monster
  • Local hospitals and clinics
  • Diagnostic imaging centers
  • Temporary staffing agencies

Qualifications for Part-Time X-Ray Tech Jobs

In order to work as an X-ray tech, you must have a high school diploma or equivalent and complete a radiologic technology program. Most programs take two years to complete and include coursework in anatomy, medical terminology, and patient care. You must also be licensed by your state in order to work as an X-ray tech.
