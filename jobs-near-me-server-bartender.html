Jobs Near Me: Becoming A Server Bartender

Introduction

Are you looking for a job that allows you to interact with people while earning a decent income? Look no further than becoming a server bartender! In this article, we'll discuss the ins and outs of this exciting career path, including what it takes to get started, the skills you'll need to succeed, and the opportunities available to you.

What is a Server Bartender?

A server bartender is a hospitality professional who serves drinks and food to customers in a restaurant or bar setting. Unlike traditional bartenders, server bartenders are responsible for taking food orders, serving meals, and clearing tables in addition to mixing and serving drinks. This dynamic role requires excellent communication skills, multitasking abilities, and a friendly demeanor.

How to Get Started

To become a server bartender, you'll typically need a high school diploma or equivalent. Some employers may require additional education or training, such as a culinary or hospitality degree. You'll also need to obtain a food handler's permit and a liquor license, which involves passing an exam and meeting state-specific requirements.

Skills Needed to Succeed

In addition to the necessary qualifications, there are several skills that will help you excel as a server bartender. These include: - Customer service: As a server bartender, you'll be interacting with customers throughout your shift. A friendly and welcoming attitude is essential to ensuring a positive dining experience. - Multitasking: From taking orders to mixing drinks to serving meals, server bartenders need to be able to handle multiple tasks simultaneously. - Attention to detail: Making sure orders are accurate and drinks are mixed to perfection is crucial in this role. - Teamwork: Working effectively with other servers, kitchen staff, and bartenders is essential to ensuring a smooth and efficient dining experience.

Opportunities Available

Server bartenders can find work in a variety of settings, including restaurants, bars, and hotels. Depending on the establishment, you may be able to work full or part-time, and some places offer flexible scheduling to accommodate school or other commitments. Additionally, many server bartenders earn tips in addition to their hourly wage, which can add up to a significant amount of extra income.

Conclusion

Becoming a server bartender is an excellent career choice for anyone who enjoys interacting with people, has excellent multitasking abilities, and thrives in a fast-paced environment. With the right qualifications, skills, and attitude, you can find exciting and rewarding work in this growing field. So, what are you waiting for? Start your journey to becoming a server bartender today!
