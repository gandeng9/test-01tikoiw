Introduction

Rossa is a name that needs no introduction in the Indonesian music industry. Born on October 9, 1978, in Sumedang, West Java, Rossa is a singer and songwriter who has won the hearts of millions of fans across Indonesia and beyond. With her soulful voice and captivating stage presence, Rossa has become one of the most popular and successful pop artists in the country.

Early Life and Career

Rossa started her music career in the mid-1990s as a member of the girl group, Ratu. However, it was her solo career that brought her to the limelight. In 2000, she released her debut album, "Nada-Nada Cinta," which was an instant success. The album sold over one million copies and established Rossa as a solo artist.

Top Hits

Over the years, Rossa has released many hit songs, including "Ayat-Ayat Cinta," "Pudar," "Tegar," and "Hati Yang Terpilih." Her songs are known for their emotional lyrics, beautiful melodies, and Rossa's amazing voice. She has won numerous awards for her music, including Best Female Pop Singer at the Anugerah Musik Indonesia (AMI) Awards.

Collaborations

Rossa has collaborated with many famous artists in Indonesia and beyond. She has worked with Malaysian singer, Hafiz, on the hit song "Salahkah." She has also collaborated with Indonesian pop star, Afgan, on the song "Kamu Yang Kutunggu." Rossa's collaborations with other artists have produced some of her biggest hits.

Philanthropy

Apart from her music, Rossa is also known for her philanthropy work. She is a UNICEF Ambassador for Indonesia and has been involved in many charity events. In 2017, she donated a portion of the proceeds from her concert to help victims of the Mount Agung eruption in Bali. Rossa is also involved in many other charitable causes, including education and health.

Personal Life

Rossa is a private person and does not talk much about her personal life. She was married to Yoyo Padi, a guitarist, in 2004, but they divorced in 2010. Rossa has two children, a son and a daughter, from her marriage.

Legacy

Rossa's impact on the Indonesian music industry cannot be overstated. She has inspired many young artists with her music and has helped to shape the pop music landscape in the country. Rossa's legacy will continue to live on for many years to come.

Conclusion

Rossa is an icon in the Indonesian music industry. Her soulful voice, emotional lyrics, and captivating stage presence have won the hearts of millions of fans across the country and beyond. With her philanthropy work and collaborations with other artists, Rossa has become more than just a singer but also a role model for many Indonesians. Her legacy will continue to inspire future generations of artists and fans.
