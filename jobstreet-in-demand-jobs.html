Introduction

Jobstreet is one of the leading online job portals in Southeast Asia. It connects employers and job seekers across different industries and job functions. As we enter the year 2023, the job market continues to evolve, and some jobs are in higher demand than others. In this article, we will explore the top in-demand jobs on Jobstreet in 2023.

1. Data Scientist

Data science is a field that involves analyzing and interpreting complex data to identify patterns and trends. As more companies rely on data to make business decisions, the demand for data scientists continues to grow. According to Jobstreet, data scientists are among the top in-demand jobs in 2023. Data scientists typically have a background in mathematics, statistics, or computer science.

2. Cybersecurity Analyst

As businesses become more reliant on technology, the risk of cyber attacks increases. Cybersecurity analysts are responsible for protecting computer networks and systems from hackers and other cyber threats. Jobstreet predicts that cybersecurity analysts will be in high demand in 2023. Cybersecurity analysts typically have a background in computer science or information technology.

3. Software Developer

Software developers are responsible for designing, developing, and testing software applications. The demand for software developers continues to grow as more businesses rely on technology to streamline their operations. According to Jobstreet, software developers will be in high demand in 2023. Software developers typically have a background in computer science or software engineering.

4. Digital Marketer

Digital marketing involves using online channels such as social media, email, and search engines to promote products or services. As more businesses shift their focus to online marketing, the demand for digital marketers continues to grow. Jobstreet predicts that digital marketers will be in high demand in 2023. Digital marketers typically have a background in marketing, communications, or business.

5. Healthcare Professional

The healthcare industry continues to grow as the population ages and new medical technologies emerge. As a result, healthcare professionals are in high demand. Jobstreet predicts that healthcare professionals, such as nurses and doctors, will continue to be in high demand in 2023. Healthcare professionals typically have a background in medicine, nursing, or allied health.

6. Artificial Intelligence (AI) Engineer

AI engineers are responsible for designing and developing artificial intelligence systems and applications. As more businesses incorporate AI into their operations, the demand for AI engineers continues to grow. According to Jobstreet, AI engineers will be in high demand in 2023. AI engineers typically have a background in computer science, mathematics, or engineering.

7. Environmental Scientist

Environmental scientists are responsible for studying the natural environment and identifying ways to protect it. As concerns about climate change and sustainability continue to grow, the demand for environmental scientists is increasing. Jobstreet predicts that environmental scientists will be in high demand in 2023. Environmental scientists typically have a background in environmental science, biology, or chemistry.

8. Supply Chain Manager

Supply chain managers are responsible for overseeing the process of getting products from the manufacturer to the consumer. As businesses become more global and supply chains become more complex, the demand for supply chain managers continues to grow. Jobstreet predicts that supply chain managers will be in high demand in 2023. Supply chain managers typically have a background in business, logistics, or supply chain management.

9. Human Resources Manager

Human resources managers are responsible for recruiting and managing employees within an organization. As businesses become more focused on employee engagement and retention, the demand for human resources managers continues to grow. Jobstreet predicts that human resources managers will be in high demand in 2023. Human resources managers typically have a background in human resources, business, or psychology.

10. Financial Analyst

Financial analysts are responsible for analyzing financial data and providing insights to help businesses make informed decisions. As businesses become more data-driven, the demand for financial analysts continues to grow. Jobstreet predicts that financial analysts will be in high demand in 2023. Financial analysts typically have a background in finance, accounting, or economics.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the job market continues to evolve, and some jobs are in higher demand than others. According to Jobstreet, the top in-demand jobs in 2023 include data scientist, cybersecurity analyst, software developer, digital marketer, healthcare professional, AI engineer, environmental scientist, supply chain manager, human resources manager, and financial analyst. If you are considering a career change or looking for a new job, these are some of the fields to consider.
