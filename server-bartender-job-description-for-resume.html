Introduction

Are you interested in becoming a server bartender? If so, it’s important to understand the job description and key responsibilities. In this article, we’ll provide an overview of what the job entails, as well as tips for crafting a strong server bartender resume.

What is a Server Bartender?

A server bartender is a hospitality professional who serves food and drinks to guests in a restaurant or bar setting. In addition to serving customers, server bartenders must also be knowledgeable about the menu, be able to make recommendations, and handle cash and credit card transactions.

Key Responsibilities

Some of the key responsibilities of a server bartender include:
  • Taking customer orders, including food and drinks
  • Making recommendations based on customer preferences and the menu
  • Preparing and serving drinks, including beer, wine, and cocktails
  • Clearing tables and keeping the bar area clean and organized
  • Handling cash and credit card transactions and making change
  • Communicating effectively with other team members, such as cooks and managers

Skills and Qualifications

To be successful as a server bartender, it’s important to have strong communication and customer service skills. Additionally, server bartenders must be able to work in a fast-paced environment, multitask, and handle cash and credit card transactions.

Education and Training

While no formal education is required to become a server bartender, many employers prefer candidates who have a high school diploma or equivalent. Additionally, some establishments may require server bartenders to have a certification in responsible alcohol service.

Resume Tips

If you’re crafting a server bartender resume, here are some tips to keep in mind:
  • Highlight your customer service skills and experience working in a fast-paced environment
  • Include any relevant certifications, such as responsible alcohol service
  • Provide specific examples of how you have gone above and beyond to deliver exceptional service to customers
  • Use keywords from the job description to help your resume stand out to hiring managers

Conclusion

Becoming a server bartender can be a rewarding career path for those with strong communication and customer service skills. By understanding the key responsibilities and crafting a strong resume, you can increase your chances of landing a job in this exciting field.
