Steve Jobs Movie 2015 Review: A Must-Watch Film

The Plot and Cast

The Steve Jobs movie, which was released in 2015, is a biographical drama film that tells the story of the life and career of the iconic Apple co-founder, Steve Jobs. The movie is based on Walter Isaacson's biography of Steve Jobs and is directed by Danny Boyle, with a screenplay by Aaron Sorkin.

Michael Fassbender plays the lead role of Steve Jobs, and he is supported by an incredible cast, including Kate Winslet, Seth Rogen, Jeff Daniels, and Michael Stuhlbarg. The movie is divided into three acts, each taking place backstage before one of Jobs' major product launches - the Macintosh in 1984, the NeXT Computer in 1988, and the iMac in 1998.

The Director's Vision

Danny Boyle's direction is superb, and he manages to keep the audience engaged throughout the movie. He uses a unique style of cinematography that gives the film a distinctive look and feel. The use of close-ups and long takes adds to the intensity of the movie and draws the audience in, making them feel like they are a part of the action.

The Screenplay

Aaron Sorkin's screenplay is a masterpiece, and it is one of the highlights of the movie. The dialogue is fast-paced, witty, and engaging, and it captures the essence of Steve Jobs perfectly. Sorkin's screenplay is also a masterclass in storytelling, and he manages to weave together the different strands of Jobs' life to create a cohesive and compelling narrative.

The Performances

Michael Fassbender delivers an outstanding performance as Steve Jobs. He manages to capture Jobs' charisma, his intensity, and his drive, making the audience believe that they are watching the real Steve Jobs on screen. Kate Winslet, who plays Joanna Hoffman, Jobs' confidante, and marketing executive, also delivers a fantastic performance. Her chemistry with Fassbender is palpable, and she brings a warmth and humanity to the character.

Seth Rogen, who plays Steve Wozniak, the co-founder of Apple, also delivers a strong performance. He manages to convey Wozniak's frustration with Jobs, as well as the deep respect and admiration he has for him. Jeff Daniels, who plays John Sculley, Apple's former CEO, also delivers a powerful performance, and his scenes with Fassbender are some of the best in the movie.

The Music

The movie's music, which is composed by Daniel Pemberton, is also worth mentioning. The score is energetic, and it complements the fast-paced nature of the movie perfectly. Pemberton's use of synthesizers and electronic instruments gives the music a futuristic feel, which is appropriate given the subject matter of the film.

The Verdict

The Steve Jobs movie is a must-watch film for anyone who is interested in the life and career of Steve Jobs. The movie is a masterclass in storytelling, and it manages to capture the essence of Jobs' life and career in a compelling and engaging way. The direction, screenplay, performances, and music are all outstanding, making this one of the best movies of 2015.

Overall Rating: 9/10
