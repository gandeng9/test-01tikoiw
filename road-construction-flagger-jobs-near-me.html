Road Construction Flagger Jobs Near Me

Introduction

If you're looking for a job in the road construction industry, becoming a flagger might be a great option for you. Flaggers are responsible for directing traffic in work zones, ensuring the safety of workers and drivers alike. In this article, we'll discuss everything you need to know about road construction flagger jobs near you.

What is a Flagger?

A flagger is a person who directs traffic in work zones. They are responsible for ensuring the safety of workers and drivers in the area. Flaggers use hand signals, flags, and signs to communicate with drivers and redirect traffic as needed.

Job Duties

As a flagger, your primary job duty is to control traffic flow in a work zone. You'll need to communicate clearly with drivers and be able to react quickly to changing situations. Other duties may include setting up and taking down work zone signs and equipment, maintaining a safe work area, and reporting any safety hazards to your supervisor.

Training and Certification

To become a flagger, you'll need to complete a training program and obtain certification from your state. The training program will cover topics such as traffic control procedures, safety guidelines, and flagging techniques. Certification typically lasts for three years and must be renewed before it expires.

Requirements

To become a flagger, you'll need to meet certain requirements. These may vary by state, but generally include being at least 18 years old, having a valid driver's license, and passing a background check. You'll also need to be able to stand for long periods of time and work in all weather conditions.

Salary and Benefits

The salary for flaggers varies by location and employer, but generally ranges from $10 to $20 per hour. Benefits may include health insurance, paid time off, and retirement plans. Some employers may also offer training and advancement opportunities.

Job Outlook

The job outlook for flaggers is positive, with the Bureau of Labor Statistics projecting a 6% increase in employment from 2022 to 2032. This is due to ongoing road construction projects and the need for traffic control in work zones.

Job Search

To find road construction flagger jobs near you, start by checking job search websites such as Indeed, Monster, and CareerBuilder. You can also contact local road construction companies and ask about job openings. Don't forget to check with your state's department of transportation for information on upcoming projects.

Conclusion

Becoming a road construction flagger can be a rewarding and exciting career choice. With the right training and certification, you can help ensure the safety of workers and drivers on the road. Use the tips in this article to start your job search today.
