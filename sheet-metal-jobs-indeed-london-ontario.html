Introduction

Are you looking for a career in sheet metal in London Ontario? You're in luck! There are plenty of job opportunities available for skilled sheet metal workers in the city. In this article, we'll explore some of the best sheet metal jobs available on Indeed.

What is Sheet Metal?

Sheet metal workers fabricate, install, and maintain heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) systems, as well as roofs, siding, and gutters. They work with materials such as aluminum, copper, brass, and steel.

What Skills Do You Need?

To become a sheet metal worker, you'll need a combination of technical skills and physical dexterity. You'll need to be able to read blueprints and schematics, operate hand and power tools, and work with precision. You'll also need to be physically fit and able to work in confined spaces and at heights.

Top Sheet Metal Jobs in London Ontario

1. Sheet Metal Installer

Sheet metal installers are responsible for installing HVAC systems in residential and commercial buildings. They work with ductwork, air handlers, and other components to ensure proper air flow and temperature control. The average salary for a sheet metal installer in London Ontario is $25-$35 per hour.

2. Sheet Metal Fabricator

Sheet metal fabricators create custom sheet metal parts for HVAC systems, roofing, and other applications. They use hand and power tools to cut, shape, and bend metal to precise specifications. The average salary for a sheet metal fabricator in London Ontario is $20-$30 per hour.

3. HVAC Technician

HVAC technicians install, maintain, and repair heating and cooling systems in residential and commercial buildings. They work with sheet metal ductwork, as well as other components such as furnaces, air conditioners, and thermostats. The average salary for an HVAC technician in London Ontario is $25-$35 per hour.

How to Find Sheet Metal Jobs in London Ontario

1. Use Online Job Boards

Indeed is a great place to start your job search. You can search for sheet metal jobs in London Ontario and filter by location, salary, and job type. You can also set up job alerts to receive notifications when new jobs are posted.

2. Check Company Websites

Many companies post job openings on their websites. Look for local HVAC and sheet metal companies in London Ontario and visit their career pages to see if they're hiring.

3. Network with Industry Professionals

Attend trade shows, conferences, and other industry events to meet other sheet metal professionals. You can also join professional associations like the Sheet Metal Workers' International Association to network and stay up-to-date on industry news.

Conclusion

In conclusion, sheet metal is a lucrative and rewarding career in London Ontario. With job opportunities in installation, fabrication, and HVAC, there's something for everyone. Use online job boards, check company websites, and network with industry professionals to find the best sheet metal job for you. Good luck with your job search!
