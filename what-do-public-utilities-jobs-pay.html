What Do Public Utilities Jobs Pay?

Introduction

Public utilities jobs refer to the roles that are responsible for the provision of basic services such as water, gas, and electricity to the public. These jobs are critical to society, as they ensure that people have access to necessary utilities that are essential to the quality of life. If you are considering a career in the public utilities sector, one of the most important factors to consider is the salary. In this article, we will explore what public utilities jobs pay in 2023.

Average Salaries in the Public Utilities Sector

According to data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), the average annual wage for employees in the utilities sector was $79,110 in 2021. This figure includes all workers in the sector, from entry-level positions to executive roles. However, it is important to note that salaries can vary widely depending on the specific job and location.

Entry-Level Salaries

For entry-level positions such as meter readers, customer service representatives, and administrative assistants, the average salary is typically lower than the overall average for the sector. According to Glassdoor, the average salary for a customer service representative in the utilities sector is $34,000 per year, while the average salary for an administrative assistant is $40,000 per year.

Skilled Trades

If you are interested in working in the skilled trades, such as electricians or plumbers, you can expect to earn a higher salary than entry-level positions. According to the BLS, the average annual wage for electricians in the utilities sector was $68,460 in 2021, while the average annual wage for plumbers was $60,160.

Management and Executive Roles

For those interested in management or executive roles, the salaries can be significantly higher. According to Glassdoor, the average salary for a utility manager is $105,000 per year, while the average salary for a vice president of operations is $160,000 per year.

Factors That Affect Salary

There are several factors that can impact the salary you can expect to earn in the public utilities sector. These include:

Location

Salaries can vary widely depending on the location of the job. For example, salaries in urban areas tend to be higher than in rural areas.

Education and Experience

Higher levels of education and experience can lead to higher salaries. For example, someone with a master's degree in engineering may earn a higher salary than someone with only a high school diploma.

Union Membership

Many public utilities jobs are unionized, which can lead to higher salaries and better benefits.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the salary you can expect to earn in the public utilities sector will depend on the specific job you are interested in, as well as factors such as location, education, and experience. While salaries for entry-level positions may be lower than the overall average for the sector, there are opportunities for advancement and higher salaries in skilled trades and management roles. If you are interested in a career in the public utilities sector, it is important to research your specific job and location to determine the salary you can expect to earn.
