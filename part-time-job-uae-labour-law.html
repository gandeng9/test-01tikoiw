Introduction

With the advent of the gig economy, part-time jobs have become increasingly popular in the UAE. However, before taking up a part-time job, it is essential to understand the legal framework that governs such employment in the country. This article provides an overview of UAE labour law with respect to part-time jobs.

Definition of Part-Time Employment

According to UAE labour law, part-time employment refers to any job where the employee works less than eight hours a day or less than 48 hours a week. The employee is entitled to all the benefits and protections afforded to full-time employees, although some benefits may be pro-rated based on the number of hours worked.

Minimum Wage

As of 2023, the minimum wage in the UAE is AED 4,000 per month. This applies to both full-time and part-time employees, although the minimum wage may be pro-rated for part-time workers. Employers are required to pay their employees at least once a month in the UAE.

Working Hours and Overtime

Part-time employees are entitled to the same working hours and rest periods as full-time employees. However, if a part-time employee works more than their designated hours, they are entitled to overtime pay at a rate of 125% of their normal hourly wage.

Leave Entitlements

Part-time employees are entitled to the same leave entitlements as full-time employees, although these may be pro-rated based on the number of hours worked. This includes annual leave, sick leave, and maternity leave.

End of Service Benefits

Part-time employees are entitled to end-of-service benefits based on the length of their employment. This includes a gratuity payment, which is calculated as 21 days of basic pay for each year of service for the first five years, and 30 days of basic pay for each subsequent year.

Termination of Employment

Employers are required to provide notice of termination to their employees, regardless of whether they are full-time or part-time. The notice period is determined by the length of service, with a minimum of 30 days for employees who have worked for less than five years, and up to 90 days for those who have worked for more than five years.

Employment Contracts

Employers are required to provide their employees with a written employment contract, which outlines the terms and conditions of employment, including salary, working hours, and leave entitlements. This contract must be in Arabic, although a translated copy may be provided in another language.

Conclusion

In conclusion, part-time employment is governed by the same labour laws as full-time employment in the UAE. Employers are required to provide their employees with the same benefits and protections, although these may be pro-rated based on the number of hours worked. Before taking up a part-time job, it is essential to understand your rights and responsibilities under UAE labour law.
